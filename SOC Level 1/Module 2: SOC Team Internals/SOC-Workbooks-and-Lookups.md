# SOC Workbooks and Lookups

## Learning Objectives
- Understand the purpose of SOC workbooks
- Learn how identity and asset inventories support investigations
- Recognize the value of network diagrams during alert triage
- Practice building investigation workflows

## Notes
- **Identity inventory** provides user information such as roles, permissions, and contact details.
- **Asset inventory** tracks systems, servers, and workstations to provide device context.
- **Network diagrams** help analysts understand network layouts, subnets, and attack paths.
- **SOC workbooks** (playbooks/runbooks) provide standardized investigation and remediation procedures.
- Workbooks generally follow three stages:
  - **Enrichment:** Gather user, asset, and threat intelligence.
  - **Investigation:** Analyze logs and determine if activity is legitimate or malicious.
  - **Escalation:** Escalate confirmed threats or communicate with affected users when necessary.

## Lab
- Built investigation workbooks using an interactive workflow exercise.
- Applied identity lookups, asset lookups, and network context to simulated alert investigations.

## Conclusion
- Learned how standardized workbooks and contextual lookups improve investigation consistency, accuracy, and efficiency during SOC alert triage.
